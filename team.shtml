Who we are

  • Becky Miller

    Co-organiser

    Becky never expected to work in government. She joined central Government in 2018 as Policy Lab's first service designer, and has been exploring and testing what it means to be in public and civil service ever since. She has worked at MHCLG in the Local Digital Collaboration Unit, and for the last 4 years at the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (Defra) helping to set up their first policy design lab for farming policy.

    At Policy Lab she led on the design of the Policy Profession’s People Centred Policy Design course on FutureLearn. In 2019, she launched the x-govt Climate Work Show and Tells with Ben Carpenter in response to the climate emergency.

    Before joining government, Becky worked in multiple small businesses and service organisations including London’s award winning music venue, Union Chapel. She enjoys blogging about her experiences as a public servant, volunteering locally and riding her bicycle.

  • Linda O'Halloran

    Co-organiser

    Linda has spent most of the last 15 years working at the ‘digital policy’ interface of central and local government in various roles at Government Digital Service, Ministry for Housing, Communities and Local Government (MHCLG), and more lately at TPXimpact.

    She has worked on collaborative digital ‘interventions’ aiming to solve common local public service challenges since 2012, leading the first Local Digital common product pilots at MHCLG, then the ‘Verify Local’ common patterns for local government at GDS. She led the Local Digital Declaration movement, set up MHCLG’s Local Digital programme and Fund in 2018, and later led the TPXimpact delivery partnership with the same programme.

    These experiences have led her to understand some of Local Government’s biggest challenges as only addressable at a national, systemic level, and government policy teams as key to overcoming them, hence her interest in supporting Policy Camp!

    Outside of Government roles, Linda has led development planning work Haiti, product development for the first release of Uniti Network’s service in Ghana, and circular economy systems design projects./p>

  • David Durant

    Co-organiser

    David is a long-time supporter of the government technology and transformation communities, starting with National Hack the Government Day and Young Rewired State back in 2009.

    Since then he has worked for mySociety, the Greater London Authority, HackIT (Hackney Council digital team) and most notably spent five years working for the Government Digital Service.

    David has been on the committee for GovCamp, the UK’s largest government-focused unconference, for several years, focusing on volunteer management.

    His main hopes for TransformGov Talks are (1) to ensure there is a good mix of future proposals and examples of real-world transformative delivery and (2) to reinvigorate the in-person government transformation community, which he feels has been lacking since the pandemic.

  • Gavin Freeguard

    Co-organiser

    Gavin has spent most of the last decade thinking about how better use of data and digital could help make government work better for the people it serves.

    A freelancer, he was previously programme director at the Institute for Government (where he is still an associate), and is policy associate at Connected by Data, special adviser at the Open Data Institute and member of the Public Digital network.

    He has also done work for organisations including mySociety, Full Fact, the Ada Lovelace Institute and Apolitical, and in previous lives worked for the Labour Party, the Media Standards Trust and the Orwell Prize.

    He is also a member of several advisory groups to government around data and information and the steering group of the Open Government Network. He tries to make data fun through the Data Bites event series and the ODI’s occasional data gameshow, the Data Game.

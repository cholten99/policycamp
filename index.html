What it is

What is Policy Camp?

Policy Camp is a one-day unconference for policy practitioners who want to improve how policy is developed and delivered in practice.

Aimed at people working in policy roles across the civil service, and those in local government, academia and the third sector, it brings together people working on real policy challenges across government and beyond to share experiences, explore what's working (and what isn't), and generate practical ideas they can take back into their roles.

Format

How an unconference works

An unconference is a participant-led format where the agenda is shaped by the people in the room.

You can take part in whatever way feels most useful to you:

  • Propose a topic you want to explore and facilitate a discussion
  • Contribute to discussions
  • Or simply join sessions that are relevant to your work

While there's no fixed agenda, the structure is designed to quickly surface the issues that matter most to participants, so discussions stay focused and practical.

What you'll get from Policy Camp

  • Relevant discussions shaped by current policy challenges
  • Practical insight and new ideas from peers facing similar challenges
  • Connections with others working on related problems across government and beyond
  • Space to explore challenges openly
